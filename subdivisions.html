Sub-divisions

What are sub-divisions?

The Los Angeles police Department operates sub departments called sub divisions. These divisions are specialized in handling a variety of unusual situations that come up in the city of Los Angeles. These sub divisions are operated by officers in the department. Some LAPD Officers are specially trained in these subdivisions to handle and carry out situations that would be more difficult for a normal Officer to handle. All subdivisions will have different SOP's and rosters that you need to follow and abide by. As well each sub division will have a different hiring process for what rank you can be at to apply.

Traffic Enforcement Unit

The Los Angeles Police Department Traffic Enforcement Unit (LAPD TEU) intends to be a unit of highly trained officers who specialize in all matters traffic related. Members are expected to, not only act with the utmost professionalism and decorum while on patrol, but to be proficient in multiple tactics and methods that can be employed to keep the roads safe.

Certifications:

  • H.E.A.T (Highway Enforcement of Aggressive Traffic)
  • MBU (MotorBike Unit)
  • Off-road
  • Bicycle Unit
  • DUI Checkpoint

Bureau of Air & Coastal Operations (BAC)

The Bureau of Air & Coastal is formed of unique & specialized units that drive to provide the best quality of service in Los Angeles & Surrounding Agencies. This assistance can be provided through aerial assets, vessels, and even all-terrain vehicles. The BAC has the ability to conduct Search & Rescue missions, vessel inspections, aerial service, and much more.

Certifications:

  • Boarding Team
  • Search and Rescue
  • Advanced Pilot

Special Weapons & Tactics (S.W.A.T)

SWAT is a designated law enforcement team, whose members are recruited, selected, trained, equipped and assigned to resolve critical incidents involving a threat to public safety which would otherwise exceed the capabilities of traditional law enforcement first responders and/or investigative units. The primary purpose of SWAT is to provide a systematic approach to saving lives in accordance with the priorities of life and the specific standards set forth in the SOP, in concert with the totality of circumstances presented. While life safety is a priority of SWAT, the specific circumstances will dictate the level of force necessary to adequately protect the public and the officers involved. Resolution of some incidents may require the specific application of various levels of force, up to and including, deadly force.

Certifications:

  • SHIELD
  • Heavy Weapons
  • Marksman
  • TASU

Criminal Investigations Division (CID)

Criminal Investigations Division is a specialized division that is responsible for the apprehension of criminals and containment of crime. Members in this division are specially trained to investigate more sophisticated crimes like homicides, murders, investigations, and robberies. Members in this division are also responsible for the enforcement of guns and drugs

Certifications:

  • Undercover
  • Narcotics
  • Raid Team

Gang Intelligence Unit (GIU)

The Gang Intelligence unit is responsible for the containment and enforcement of gang related activities and crimes. GIU works closely with other gang suppression units in other agencies to provide more interdepartmental relations to provide more efficiency in the containment of crimes.

Certifications:

  • Undercover
  • W.I.U
  • Raid Team

K9 Division (K9)

The LAPD K9 Division is a specialized Unit prioritizing in Search and Rescue (SAR), apprehension of Fleeing Suspects and detecting Contraband, making use of trained Police K9 dogs in the case that they would outperform a Human Officer, for example in a foot chase. In the case of Suspect Apprehension, dogs may be used to more efficiently and quickly incapacitate a Suspect. In the case of Contraband Detection, the K9 Officer may utilize the dog for a “contraband sniff” to either ensure the lack of contraband or create probable cause for a full search.

Certifications:

  • Search and Rescue

Sandy Division

Sandy Division enables Officers in the LAPD to go out of there normal patrol zone and patrol in the County assisting other agencies like BCSO. With the Sandy Division in place, this also allows some officers in the LAPD to work with other departments.

Certifications:

  • Wildlife Officer
